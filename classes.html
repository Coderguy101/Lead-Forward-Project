What Will I Learn?

Many prospective students wonder what they will learn when pursuing a Computer Science degree. Below are some of the key core classes you will take here at Clemson and a brief description of each course. I will also list some helpful tips on some of the critical skills you will use throughout your major to help you decide whether or not computer science is something you want to do.

Core Classes

These are some of the key classes you will take within your first 2-3 years studing Computer Science

Introduction To Computer Science Sequence

In your Intro to Computer Science Sequence, you will be introduced to fundamental programming techniques essential to any programming language. These could include things like loops, boolean logic, object-oriented programing, etc. You will also be introduced to more advanced topics such as inheritance, polymorphism, and pointers. You will learn things quick in your intro sequence, and it is crucial you do not fall behind.

Algorithms and Data Structures

This class is usually taken after your Intro to Computer Science Sequence. In this course, you will learn about data structures and algorithms that combine many of the concepts and techniques you have learned. You will have far more complicated programming assignments in this course, so make sure to start them early. Try to understand the different data structures and how they work (Stacks, Maps, Trees, Etc) very well because topics from this course will likely be tested during your technical interviews.

Intro to Computer Organization

This course introduces you to the underlying logic of how computers function. You will be introduced to more advanced boolean logic in this class, and you will also learn some basic assembly language commands. Additionally, you will do an extensive C review, intensively studying pointers.

View Recent Syllabus

Software Developmental Foundations

This course introduces you to what it means to be a Software Developer rather than simply a programmer. You will intensively study some very fundamental design principles on what it means to be a good programmer, such as coding to the interface, Separation of Concerns, Design by Contract, etc. You will walk away from this course with much better coding practices. This course is a lot of work, and make sure to start your projects in this class early.

View Recent Syllabus

Linear Algebra

Linear Algebra is the mathematical study of vectors and matrices. Linear algebra is the most helpful field of mathematics in Computer Science, as it is used in many different fields. You will learn valuable concepts, such as linear transformations, vector spaces, eigenvectors, and much more, and go over examples on how linear algebra can be applied to other fields, such as computing.

Intro To Operating Systems

Intro To Operating Systems is regarded as one of the most challenging classes in the entire CS major. In this course, you will intensively study Operating Systems to develop and understand how exactly the hardware and software interact. Make sure to have a good professor for this course, as it is one of the most important classes you will take.

Is Computer Science For Me?

While the question of whether or not you should study Computer Science is going to ultimately be a decision you must make, here are some key skills you will need as a Computer Scientist to help you make an informed decision.

Great Logical and Problem Solving Skills

If you've always been the type of person who naturally was drawn to math and science, then Computer Science may be for you. Computer Science is all about problem-solving, and you will have to be able to work through large-scale projects in your career diligently. Nobody writes perfect code on their first try, and having a solid ability to logically trace back and understand every aspect of your code will be critical for you to know.

The Ability To Quickly Adapt To Changing Enviornments

It is no secret that the tech industry is one of the fastest-changing industries to go into. Change is inevitable in Computer Science; it's not about if change will happen; it is about when change will happen and, more importantly, how you will respond to change. Throughout your coursework at Clemson, you will be expected to quickly pick up new languages with little instruction (C++, Java, C, python, etc) and quickly apply often abstract concepts to numerous coding assignments.

A Passion to Understand How Computer's function at their core

While being interested in the field may seem obvious, you would be surprised at how many people go into Computer Science specifically because they think it will be relatively easy and pay well. This is false, Computer Science is a complex field and while it does pay well, if you are not at least somewhat interested in your work, you will not get far. Computer Science is currently the major with the highest dropout rate; it is crucial you have some underlying passion for Computing.
